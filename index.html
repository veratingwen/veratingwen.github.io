Project overview

Challenges

The topic of 'active journey' has been given in my master’s study, which is one of the UN development goals. The government and the UN want to increase the proportion of active travel. However, the topic was very broad so we had a hard time to narrow our design direction. 

After several rounds of researching and brainstorming, we focused on people who go to the supermarket. It was because we found that many people still choose to drive to the supermarket within a 5-10 minute walking distance. However, how to offer a fun and low-carbon solution was a challenge for us.

Objectives

The target we set was to start in the Scottish Highlands and use Tesco, the only major supermarket in that area, as a pilot. The project is hoped to attract shoppers' attention and increase the percentage of people on Active Journey.

01 Further research

Research plan

Research enables us to dig into our understanding of users - not only their immediate frustrations, but also their hopes, fears, abilities, limitations, reasoning, and goals. It lays essential foundations for creating solutions in later stages.

To ensure the research stays on track and better guide our design later, it is important to create a research plan before diving into the research phase. I listed research goals, research questions, assumptions, methodologies, participants, and timeline in our research plan.

Research goals

  • Understand the target users' habits
  • Identify the reasons why people want to travel by car
  • Learn about the preferences, frustrations, goals, needs, motiva-tions of target groups

Methodologies

  • Secondary Research (Market Research, Desk Research)
  • Primary Research (Shadowing, Field Interview, Engagement Tool)

Second research

We did some desk research that included in terms of why people travelled, the proportion of journeys made by car, perceptions of public transport in Scotland and how often people went to the supermarket.

01/ Shopping is a major reason why people travel

People travel for various purposes, with 26.6% travelling for shopping, the highest percentage.

02/ The highest percentage of travel is by car

65% of people travelling by car, which reported in 'Transport and Travel in Scotland 2019'.

03/ Attitudes of people in Scotland towards public transport

ONLY 40%

of people in rural areas are ‘satisfied’ the delivered public transport service.

Attitudes towards public transport directly impact people's choice of travel mode.

04/ Shopping patterns lead to carry problem

61% of households shop once to twice a week.

Shopping as a family will purchase many ingredients, so they need to carry shopping bags. This is one of the reasons why shoppers drive to do their shopping.

Primary research

Shadowing

We went to Tesco and observed the shoppers, to explore what methods people use to go shopping, the ratio of driving to car/walking, and people's shopping habits.

Field interview

Duration: 15 - 20 mins / one person
Participant: Customers who go shopping at Tesco

The participants are all shoppers who shop at Tesco. Based on the interviews, we hoped to find out whether they were aware of active journeys, how often they used public transport, the factors affecting their travel style, etc.

Engagement tool

By giving users realistic scenarios. For example, what mode of travel would be used to get from home to Tesco for shopping when there are a bunch of things to buy? In this way, we can get the user engaged, reduce deliberately guided behaviour, and visualise information providing by users based on their real life experiences during the interview.

Design conception

UX Version

This is an opportunity for us to consider the status of "Active Journey" that provides a product or service to people living in Scotland's suburbs. The hope is that through a simple and effective process and a fun shopping format, people will be inspired to reduce their carbon footprint by travelling in the active journey way . At the same time, a new form of travel for people in the suburb is provided.

Product & app

Product

Product + app

  • Booking scooter + quick unlocking

    Stay updated on available scooters, book them, and quickly unlock for use using NFC technology.

  • Gamification of shopping experience

    Add fun to the shopping process and inspire people to use the scooter's active journey.

  • Efficient shopping process

    Select online, and then supermarkets get the order and pack. Users can pick it up immediately after arriving at the supermarket.
